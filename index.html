Drink Mix Experiment

In biology class, I had a brief introduction to solutes and solvents.
During the lecture, it was said that there are 3 factors that affect the rate of dissolution

  • (1) the surface area of the solute
  • (2) the temperature of the solvent
  • (3) the amount of agitation that occurs when the solute and the solvent are mixed.

    • The factor that I want to test is the temperature of the solvent.
    The water on the left is very hot to the touch, the one in the center
    room temperature (about 65-70F), and the one on the right is the ice cold water.

    Here the surface areas are the same for each solution and there is no agitation taking place to any of the solutions.
    This way, I can focus on the main factor.
